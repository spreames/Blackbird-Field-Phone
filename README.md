# Blackbird Field Phone

A cave rescue field phone for replacing the TP-6N.

#### **Status: Incomplete -- do not use until all files loaded**

### Background:

In the USA, cave rescue communications have been done with military field phones for decades. These are two-wire systems that are part of a party-line system (aka parallel connected). The National Cave Rescue Commission (NCRC) has a cache of these phones that are used for training. These units are available in the military surplus stores and online such as eBay. Over the years these field phones have become standard for all cave rescue teams in the USA.

There are three standard field phones that are in use today. The TA-1 ("sound powered" phone) which does not requires batteries but has a very limited range of 2 miles or less. The TA-312 (the "crank phone") has a theoretical range of 20 miles but weights 10.0 lbs with batteries! The TP-6N (the "NATO phone") has the same range but has solid-state circuitry, is much lighter (2.5 lbs), and much smaller.

The problem is that the units are failing. There a no replacement parts, no schematics, and no theory of operation. The most recent model, the TP-6N, was designed in 1973 -- a 50-year old design!

### Introduction to the Blackbird:

In December 2021, the NCRC formed the Communication Committee (CommComm) to address the increasing failure rate with the military phones in the field. The group decided to begin the design of a clone of the TP-6N which was named "Blackbird". Many of the original components are obsolete and very hard to obtain such as custom transformers and uni-junction transistors. So the team designed a functional clone such that works the same as the TP-6N but uses the modern techniques such as SMT components, KiCAD schematics and PCB layouts, 3D printing, and LTspice simulations.

The requirements and goals are:

- Backward compatable with the existing field phones

- Lighter, smaller, and requiring less power

- Runs off three AA batteries

- Is the same cost or less as TP-6Ns on eBay (when they are available)
